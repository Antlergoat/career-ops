## Target Role Profile

### Preferred Role Types (ranked)
1. **Creative Marketing Lead / Manager** — leads a creative team, owns brand narrative,
   bridges strategy and execution. This is the bull's-eye.
2. **Product Marketing Manager (PMM)** — at a notable company (AI, tech, creative tools,
   design platforms). Strong fit when the product has a visual/creative/brand dimension.
3. **Assistant Director / Senior Manager** — leadership scope without the full Director
   weight. Ideal step if moving into a larger organization.
4. **Marketing Manager (general)** — viable if the scope is substantive and the company
   or industry is interesting.

### Role Title Flexibility
Job titles in creative marketing are inconsistent. Evaluate on **scope and function**, not
title. Relevant titles include but are not limited to:
- Creative Marketing Manager / Lead
- Brand Marketing Manager
- Product Marketing Manager
- Content Marketing Manager (if strategic, not just execution)
- Marketing Communications Manager
- Senior Marketing Manager
- PowerPoint Designer / Presentation Designer (at tier-1 companies — see stretch policy)
- Visual Communications Lead
- Brand Strategist

### Company Tier Weighting
**Balanced — both role fit and company prestige matter equally.**
A strong role at a mid-tier company beats a weak role at a famous one. But company
prestige is a legitimate factor — being able to say "I worked at NVIDIA / Figma / Adobe"
compounds future optionality and is worth accepting some role-title compromise.

### Stretch Role Policy
When a role at a prestigious company has a title that undersells the scope (e.g., "PowerPoint
Designer" at NVIDIA, "Content Coordinator" at Figma), **flag it as a stretch but evaluate
fully**. Do not penalize the evaluation score for title mismatch alone. Look at:
- Actual responsibilities in the JD
- Team size / reporting structure signals
- Growth trajectory implied
- Whether the company name materially advances the career narrative

## Resume Routing

When generating a CV for a job description, select the appropriate base resume before
tailoring. This is a directive — read the JD signals, choose the base, then tailor from it.
Do not tailor from `cv.md` if a more targeted base exists.

| JD Signal Keywords | Base Resume to Use |
|---|---|
| "brand," "creative," "design," "visual," "content," "campaign," "storytelling," "art direction," "creative direction" | `cv-creative-lead.md` |
| "GTM," "go-to-market," "product marketing," "PMM," "launch," "personas," "positioning," "market research," "competitive intelligence" | `cv-pmm.md` |
| Tier-1 company (NVIDIA, Apple, Adobe, Figma, Google, Anthropic, OpenAI, Pixar/Disney, Rockstar, EA, or similar) + narrow or junior title + substantive JD scope | `cv-stretch.md` |
| Mixed signals, unclear JD, or none of the above apply | `cv.md` (master — fallback) |

**Selection rules:**
1. If GTM/PMM keywords and creative keywords both appear, check the role's primary function.
   Reports to a product team → use `cv-pmm.md`. Reports to brand or creative → use `cv-creative-lead.md`.
2. The tier-1/stretch override applies regardless of whether PMM or creative keywords are
   also present — prestige company context changes the framing.
3. `cv.md` is always the fallback. If signals are absent or contradictory, use it.
4. After selecting the base, tailor it to the specific JD using the proof points and
   positioning guidance in this file.
